\item
  The \(k\)th factorial moment of a random variable \(X\) is
  \(\mathbb{E} \left( \frac{X!}{(X - k)!} \right)\), assuming the
  expectation exists.

  \begin{enumerate}
  \def\labelenumii{\roman{enumii}.}
  \tightlist
  \item
    Find the second factorial moment of the Poisson distribution.
  \item
    Find the second factorial moment of the binomial distribution.
  \item
    Use your answers above to calculate the variance of each
    distribution.
  \end{enumerate}
\item
  (Exponential distribution) Let \(X \sim F(x)\) where
  \(F(x) = 1 - e^{-\alpha x}\). Find the mean and variance of \(X\).
\item
  (Truncated Poisson) Let \(X \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda)\) and define
  \(Y\) by the probability mass function \[
  P(Y = y) = \frac{P(X = y)}{P(X > 0)}
  \;,\qquad y = 1, 2, \dots
  \] Find an expression for the PMF of \(Y\), and calculate its mean and
  variance.
\item
  With \(Y\) as in the previous problem, show that
  \(\mathbb{E}\log(Y) \geq 0\).
\item
  (Probability integral transform) Let \(X\) be a continuous random
  variable with strictly increasing CDF \(F\), and let \(Y = F(X)\).
  Show that \(Y \sim \text{uniform}(0, 1)\). (If \(F\) is strictly
  increasing, then \(F^{-1}(x)\) is well-defined).
\end{enumerate}
